## Project Overview

A multi-specialty surgery center in Southeast Texas had reached end of service life on its entire surgical table fleet. Aging equipment across four ORs was generating increasing service calls and beginning to affect scheduling reliability.

Surgiquip provided a single-source solution: four Skytron 6701 surgical tables, Aurora LED lighting for all four rooms, and a structured preventive maintenance program — all phased to keep the surgery center running throughout.

## Scope of Work

- Four Skytron 6701 surgical tables — versatile positioning for general surgery, orthopedics, and laparoscopy
- Four Skytron Aurora LED surgical light systems — main and satellite heads per room, CRI 97+
- Trade-in and removal of legacy equipment coordinated with facility management
- Staggered room-by-room installation — only one OR offline per day during the transition window
- Four-room PM contract established at handoff; bi-annual visits with emergency response coverage included

## Outcome

All four rooms transitioned to new equipment within the planned window. The surgery center's biomed team reported a significant reduction in unplanned service calls. The PM contract remains active.

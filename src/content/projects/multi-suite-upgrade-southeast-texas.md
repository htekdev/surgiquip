## Project Overview

A multi-specialty surgery center in Southeast Texas had reached the end of service life on its surgical table fleet. Aging equipment was generating increasing service calls and beginning to affect scheduling reliability. The facility needed a modern, coordinated solution — not piecemeal replacements.

Surgiquip Solutions provided a single-source answer: new Skytron Advantage surgical tables, new LED surgical lighting, and a structured preventive maintenance program to protect the investment going forward.

## Scope of Work

**Equipment Replacement**
- Four Skytron Advantage 6001 surgical tables — versatile for general surgery, orthopedics, and laparoscopy
- Four Skytron Elite 6000 LED surgical light systems — main and satellite heads per room
- Trade-in and removal of legacy equipment coordinated with facility management

**Installation**
- Staggered room-by-room installation to maintain surgical schedule continuity — only one OR offline per day during a staggered installation window
- Full table leveling, calibration, and accessory commissioning
- Light positioning and focus calibration for each room's specific ceiling configuration

**PM Program Setup**
- Four-room PM contract initiated at project handoff
- Bi-annual PM visits covering tables, lights, and accessories
- Emergency service response coverage included

## Project Highlights

- **Zero scheduled OR downtime** — staggered room approach kept 3 of 4 ORs fully operational throughout the staggered transition window
- **Fleet standardization** — all four rooms on identical table platforms reduces staff retraining and spare parts complexity
- **Same-vendor PM** — factory-trained Surgiquip technicians maintain the equipment they installed

## Outcome

All four rooms were transitioned to new equipment within the planned window. Facilities consistently report a reduction in unplanned service calls following fleet standardization.

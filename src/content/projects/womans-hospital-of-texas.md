## Project Overview

Woman's Hospital of Texas is one of the largest women's specialty hospitals in the country — and one of the busiest obstetric programs in the Houston area, delivering thousands of babies annually across its 35 LDRP suites. When the hospital's existing birthing light system — supplied by a competitor — began generating persistent complaints from nursing staff, the facility turned to Surgiquip Solutions for a better solution.

The complaints were familiar: dust accumulating in the door-track mechanism, lights jamming at critical delivery moments, and maintenance staff spending hours cleaning and lubricating a system that should have been hands-off.

## The Problem

The competitor's door-mounted birthing lights were designed with track-and-pivot door mechanisms that, in practice, became maintenance and infection control liabilities:

- **Dust traps** — the track and hinge mechanisms accumulated dust and biologic debris between cleaning cycles
- **Jamming** — at critical moments during delivery, lights bound in tracks, requiring nursing staff to manually force repositioning
- **Cleaning burden** — nursing and EVS teams were spending 20+ minutes per room on light cleaning alone during room turnovers
- **Staff frustration** — the combination of unreliable positioning and cleaning demands was driving negative staff feedback on the unit

## Scope of Work

### LDRP Suites (35 Rooms)

- **Skytron AR24 Ceiling Birthing Lights** — ceiling-mounted design eliminates the door track entirely; no track, no hinge, no pivot point to accumulate debris or jam
- **Wand Control System** — non-contact light positioning using a touch wand, eliminating the need to handle the light head directly and removing the mechanical jam point
- Removal and disposal of the competitor system
- Ceiling-mount installation coordination across all 35 active LDRP suites, phased to maintain obstetric operational capacity during installation

## Project Highlights

- **Replacement project** — Surgiquip was brought in specifically to solve a problem the competitor product created, not just to outfit a new facility
- **35-room scale** — one of the largest single-facility LDRP birthing light installations in the Houston market
- **Phased active facility** — installation managed in phases to maintain obstetric capacity throughout the project
- **Root-cause solution** — ceiling-mount design eliminates the track mechanism that caused both dust accumulation and jamming, not just treating symptoms

## Outcome

Woman's Hospital of Texas completed the transition across all 35 LDRP suites with nursing staff reporting immediate improvement in light positioning reliability and room turnover time. The competitor complaint was resolved — and Surgiquip established a long-term equipment relationship with the facility.

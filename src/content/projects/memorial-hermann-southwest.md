## Project Overview

Memorial Hermann Southwest's catheterization laboratory supports interventional cardiology procedures for the Southwest Houston community. The catheterization lab environment presents a unique engineering challenge: the Siemens bi-plane C-arm imaging system — with two imaging chains rotating in a shared space — requires precise ceiling-mount coordination to maintain full imaging arc clearance while providing complete boom service reach.

Surgiquip Solutions designed and installed the ceiling-mount infrastructure specifically around the Siemens bi-plane system footprint.

## Scope of Work

### Catheterization Laboratory (1 Lab)

- **Center Mount Equipment Booms with Extended Reach Arms** — the extended arm configuration was selected specifically to provide adequate service reach at all table positions while maintaining clearance for both C-arm imaging chains of the bi-plane system
- **Aurora LED Surgical Lights** — positioned to clear the dual-plane imaging arcs while delivering procedural illumination for catheterization and device deployment

## Engineering Considerations

Bi-plane C-arm systems are among the most demanding ceiling coordination challenges in medical equipment installation:

- **Dual arc clearance** — both imaging chains must rotate freely without contact with ceiling infrastructure
- **Extended reach requirement** — boom arms must reach across the imaging zone to deliver services at all table positions
- **Aurora LED positioning** — light head positioning must clear both arcs at all rotation positions

Surgiquip coordinated the ceiling infrastructure design directly with the Siemens installation team to validate clearance at all system configurations before installation.

## Project Highlights

- **Bi-plane engineering** — custom extended-reach boom arm configuration designed specifically for the Siemens bi-plane imaging envelope
- **Imaging zone coordination** — full clearance validated at all C-arm positions before installation commenced

## Outcome

Memorial Hermann Southwest's cath lab was commissioned with full imaging arc clearance confirmed and all boom services operational. The facility runs a full interventional cardiology program for the Southwest Houston market.

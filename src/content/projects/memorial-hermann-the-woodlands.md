## Project Overview

Memorial Hermann The Woodlands Medical Center serves the Montgomery County community north of Houston, including the rapidly growing Woodlands master-planned community. When the facility's obstetrics program needed new LDRP suite lighting, Memorial Hermann turned to Surgiquip Solutions — the same team that had solved the birthing light problem at Memorial Hermann Memorial City.

The solution was identical: Skytron wand-controlled ceiling birthing lights that eliminate the dust-accumulation and mechanical jamming problems inherent to door-mounted birthing light systems.

## Scope of Work

### LDRP Suites (34 Rooms)

- **Skytron Ceiling Birthing Lights with Wand Control** — ceiling-mount design eliminates the door track and pivot mechanism that caused jamming and dust accumulation in door-mounted systems
- **Wand positioning** — non-contact light positioning control, reducing contamination risk and eliminating the mechanical point of failure
- Structural coordination for ceiling-mount installation across all 34 LDRP suites

### Emergency Department (13 Rooms)

- Ceiling-mount exam lights for emergency room clinical workflows

## Why Ceiling Birthing Lights

The Memorial City LDRP project demonstrated the value of ceiling-mount birthing lights over door-mounted alternatives. Key advantages validated at scale:

- **No dust traps** — ceiling units have no track or hinge mechanisms to accumulate debris
- **No jamming** — wand control eliminates the mechanical pivot and track contact points
- **Infection control** — wand positioning avoids direct hand contact with the light head
- **Consistent performance** — 34 suites operating to the same standard, simplifying staff training and PM scheduling

## Project Highlights

- **34-room scale** — one of the larger LDRP outfitting projects in the Houston market
- **Memorial Hermann system pattern** — consistent with the Memorial City solution, simplifying system-wide PM and parts standardization

## Outcome

Memorial Hermann The Woodlands' LDRP program and emergency department were commissioned with ceiling birthing lights and exam lights installed and validated. Nursing staff report the same improvement in usability and reduction in maintenance burden seen at the Memorial City campus.

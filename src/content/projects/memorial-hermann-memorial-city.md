## Project Overview

Memorial Hermann Memorial City Medical Center's Labor, Delivery, Recovery, and Postpartum (LDRP) floor is one of the largest and busiest obstetric programs in the Houston area. When the facility's existing door-mounted birthing light system was generating complaints — lights jamming at critical moments, dust accumulating in hard-to-clean recesses, and maintenance staff spending hours on preventive cleaning — Memorial Hermann turned to Surgiquip Solutions for a better answer.

The solution: Skytron Mini Argos ceiling-mounted birthing lights with wand control, installed across all 26 LDRP suites.

## The Problem with Traditional Birthing Lights

Door-mounted birthing lights are a common point of frustration in LDRP environments:

- **Dust accumulation** — hinge mechanisms and track systems trap dust, lint, and biologic debris in hard-to-reach areas
- **Jamming** — at critical moments during delivery, lights can bind in tracks or at pivot points
- **Cleaning burden** — staff report spending disproportionate time on light cleaning during room turnover
- **Positioning limitations** — door-mounted systems restrict positioning relative to patient and delivery team

## Scope of Work

### LDRP Suites (26 Rooms)

- **Skytron Mini Argos Ceiling Birthing Lights** — compact, ceiling-mount design eliminates the door track entirely
- **Wand control system** — allows nurses and midwives to reposition the light without touching the head, reducing contamination risk and eliminating the mechanical jamming point
- **Ceiling-mount installation** — structural coordination with the facility for ceiling mounting in all 26 suites

## Project Highlights

- **Eliminated the root cause** — ceiling-mount design removes the door track and pivot mechanism that caused jamming and dust accumulation
- **Wand control** — non-contact positioning reduces infection risk and staff frustration
- **26-room consistency** — every LDRP suite operates identically, simplifying staff orientation and PM scheduling

## Outcome

Memorial Hermann Memorial City's LDRP floor transitioned from a recurring maintenance headache to a standardized, reliable ceiling lighting system. Nursing staff reported immediate improvement in usability, and the maintenance team eliminated the repetitive dust-cleaning burden from their room turnover checklist.

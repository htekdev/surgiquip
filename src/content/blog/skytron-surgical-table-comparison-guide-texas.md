## 6000 Series — The General Surgery Workhorse

The Skytron 6000 is the foundational platform — the table that anchors a general surgery, gynecology, or urology OR and can adapt across a broad procedural range through Skytron's modular accessory system.

### Clinical Profile

The 6000 is optimized for:
- General and laparoscopic surgery (appendectomy, cholecystectomy, colorectal)
- Gynecologic procedures (hysterectomy, laparoscopic ovarian cystectomy, robotic-assisted)
- Urologic surgery (nephrectomy, cystoscopy, TURP — with cystoscopy leg attachments)
- General orthopedic (shoulder arthroplasty, joint scope procedures with positioning attachments)
- Bariatric surgery (1,000 lb capacity, wide top section)

### Positioning Range

- Trendelenburg / reverse Trendelenburg (±30° standard, up to ±40° with accessory)
- Lateral tilt (±20°)
- Flex and reflex (kidney/jack-knife position)
- Height range: 26"–42" (supports both sitting surgeon positions and floor-level patient transfers)
- One-touch memory positioning (programmable positions that return to surgeon-preferred setup)

### Imaging Considerations

The 6000 base platform is not fully radiolucent. A partial radiolucent tabletop option allows C-arm imaging over the torso and extremities but not head-to-toe full-body fluoroscopy. For facilities that need complete imaging coverage across all body regions, the 6500 or 6700 is more appropriate.

### Who Should Choose the 6000?

Community hospitals and surgery centers with general surgery, GYN, urology, and bariatric case mix — where C-arm imaging is episodic (hip fracture cases, occasional cholangiography) rather than procedure-defining — get the most ROI from the 6000 platform. At a lower price point than the specialty platforms, it is also the right choice for secondary ORs in large health systems where specialized tables cover the complex rooms and the 6000 handles day-to-day general caseload.

## 6500 Series — Spine and Orthopedic Precision

The Skytron 6500 is engineered for the positional demands of spine surgery, complex orthopedic reconstruction, and trauma — environments where precise prone positioning, full-body fluoroscopic access, and reliable attachment point geometry are procedure-defining requirements.

### Clinical Profile

The 6500 is designed for:
- Lumbar and cervical spine surgery (laminectomy, discectomy, fusion, TLIF, XLIF)
- Deformity correction and scoliosis reconstruction
- Complex trauma (pelvis, femur, tibial plateau, acetabular — with Jackson frame or Wilson frame)
- Total joint replacement (TKA, THA — with positioning attachments and contoured top sections)
- Shoulder arthroplasty in beach-chair position (with specific shoulder positioner)

### Wilson Frame Integration

The 6500 is the preferred table for Wilson frame-based prone spine positioning. The Wilson frame's saddle geometry produces thoracic flexion that decompresses the thecal sac and opens the interlaminar space — critical for access in lumbar fusion approaches. Skytron engineers the 6500 chassis mounting geometry to precisely align with Wilson frame legs, eliminating the stabilization compromises that occur when third-party frames are used with mismatched tables.

### Imaging Window

The 6500's radiolucent imaging window extends from head to toe in the prone position, providing unobstructed C-arm access for pedicle screw placement verification, rod positioning confirmation, and intraoperative plain film review. The tabletop is rated for the weight of intraoperative navigation systems that attach to the table frame.

### Positional Safety

Long-duration prone positioning cases demand tables with lock mechanisms that genuinely hold under patient weight, lateral shifting loads, and the mechanical forces applied during retraction. The 6500's multi-axis locking system is designed for spine cases that routinely exceed four to six hours.

### Who Should Choose the 6500?

Orthopedic and spine-focused hospitals, spine surgery centers, and trauma centers. Facilities in the Texas Medical Center complex, the Fondren Orthopedic Group campus, and regional spine centers serving Southeast Texas (Beaumont, Corpus Christi, Victoria) use the 6500 as their primary spine OR table.

## 6700 Series — Hybrid OR and Cardiovascular

The Skytron 6700 is the flagship platform for hybrid operating rooms, cardiovascular surgery, and any environment where a fixed-plate imaging system — biplane, cone-beam CT, O-arm — defines the clinical capability of the room.

### Clinical Profile

The 6700 is built for:
- Hybrid cardiovascular procedures: TAVR, EVAR, TEVAR, hybrid coronary revascularization (HCR)
- Structural heart: transcatheter mitral valve repair (TMVr), left atrial appendage occlusion (LAAO)
- Vascular: complex aortic endografting, fenestrated EVAR, branched-vessel work
- Neurological: endovascular stroke treatment, cerebral aneurysm coiling, AVM embolization
- Interventional cardiology in a surgical-backup environment

### Carbon Fiber Top Section

The 6700's full carbon fiber tabletop delivers complete radiolucency across all body regions without any metallic interruption that would create artifact in fluoroscopic or CT-based imaging. In a biplane hybrid OR suite — where the C-arm rotates through a full 360° sweep around the patient — any table hardware that enters the imaging field creates artifact that obscures the vascular anatomy being treated. The 6700 eliminates this problem.

### Isocenter Positioning

Isocenter positioning allows the table to translate the patient to the exact geometric center of the imaging system's field of view with programmed precision — critical for biplane systems where the intersection of the two imaging planes defines the target. The 6700 supports programmed isocenter recall, so the interventionalist and surgeon can return to a confirmed imaging position after repositioning the patient without re-shooting localizer films.

### Biplane Compatibility

Skytron engineers the 6700 specifically in partnership with major biplane manufacturers (Siemens, Philips, GE) to ensure table height, lateral extent, and base footprint don't conflict with C-arm sweep paths. For Southeast Texas hybrid ORs, this matters: a table that wasn't engineered for biplane compatibility requires compromise — either limiting imaging angulation or limiting the positions the patient can be placed in.

Any facility building or renovating a hybrid OR, or that runs a structural heart or complex endovascular program that requires biplane or rotational CT imaging. The 6700 is not the right choice for a general surgery OR — its specialized engineering comes at a premium that only yields ROI in high-imaging-volume cardiovascular or neurointerventional environments.

## 7000 Series — Advanced Spine and Neurosurgery

The Skytron 7000 represents the highest-specification platform in the lineup, designed for complex spine reconstruction, neuro-oncology, and long-duration cases that require exceptional table stability, expanded rail systems for intraoperative navigation attachment, and full-body imaging access in multiple positions.

### Clinical Profile

The 7000 serves:
- Complex adult and pediatric spine deformity (scoliosis, kyphosis, spondylolisthesis reconstruction)
- Revision spine surgery (multiple-level instrumentation removal and replacement)
- Neurosurgical cranial cases (supine, lateral, sitting position craniectomy, posterior fossa)
- Complex pelvic and acetabular reconstruction requiring full lower extremity imaging
- Intraoperative navigation-guided spine procedures (attachment points for Stealth Station, Brainlab, Medtronic systems)

### Expanded Rail System

The 7000's modular rail system supports heavier navigation system attachments, intraoperative ultrasound mounts, and self-retaining retractor systems without the table geometry compromising access. For spine surgeons who use robotic-assisted systems (ExcelsiusGPS, Globus Excelsius) or optical navigation, the 7000's rail geometry is engineered for those attachment relationships.

### Who Should Choose the 7000?

Academic medical centers, neurosurgical specialty hospitals, and spine programs with high volumes of complex deformity and revision surgery. For most community hospitals and ASCs, the 6500 provides sufficient capability at a lower investment point.

## Matching Table to Case Mix: A Decision Framework

Before selecting a Skytron model, OR directors should answer four questions:

**1. What is your imaging workflow?**
If C-arm use is occasional (< 30% of cases), the 6000 with partial radiolucency is adequate. If C-arm or biplane is present in > 50% of cases, or if you have a fixed imaging system in the room, the 6700 is required.

**2. What is your prone positioning volume?**
If spine surgery represents > 15–20% of your caseload, the 6500's prone-positioning precision and Wilson frame integration will produce measurable workflow improvement over a general-purpose table.

**3. What is your bariatric surgical volume?**
All current Skytron models are rated to 1,000 lb. However, the 6000's wider standard top section and broader accessory ecosystem for bariatric positioning make it the preferred table for facilities with significant bariatric surgery programs.

**4. What is your service infrastructure?**
A specialty table like the 6700 requires factory-trained service to maintain warranty and PM documentation standards. If your biomedical team has not received Skytron factory training, partnering with an authorized dealer (Surgiquip) for PM service is essential.

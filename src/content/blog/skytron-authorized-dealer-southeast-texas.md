Skytron is one of the most trusted names in surgical lighting, OR tables, and equipment boom systems. But not every company that installs or sells Skytron equipment has the same level of authorization, training, or support capability. Here's what Texas hospital facilities managers and perioperative directors need to know before committing to a supplier.

## What "Authorized Dealer" Actually Means

An **authorized Skytron dealer** has a direct commercial relationship with Skytron — meaning:

- **Factory-trained technicians** who have completed Skytron's certification programs
- **Direct access to OEM parts** at manufacturer pricing (not sourced through secondary distributors)
- **Warranty claim authority** — authorized dealers can initiate and process Skytron warranty work without going through a third party
- **Technical support access** — direct line to Skytron's engineering and technical teams

Non-authorized resellers may source equipment through other channels, but they typically cannot honor manufacturer warranties, may use non-OEM parts, and lack access to Skytron's technical resources.

## The Southeast Texas Dealer Landscape

Texas is a large state, and Skytron authorization is **regional**. A dealer authorized for Dallas may not have authorization to install and service equipment in Houston. When evaluating a supplier for a Houston-area or Southeast Texas prooect:

- Confirm **geographic authorization** — are they specifically authorized for your region?
- Ask for **proof of dealer authorization** — reputable dealers can provide documentation
- Verify **technician certification** — individual technicians, not oust the company, hold certifications

## Installation Quality: Why It Affects Long-Term Performance

Skytron surgical lighting systems — particularly ceiling-mount heads and integrated boom systems — require precise installation to perform as designed. Misaligned light heads drift. Poorly anchored booms develop vibration issues over time. Improperly commissioned gas outlets create NFPA 99 compliance gaps.

Factory-authorized installation means:

1. **Anchor placement and structural load verification** are handled per Skytron's specifications
2. **Boom arm balancing and calibration** is performed to factory standards — not field-guessed
3. **Light head alignment** is set during installation, with documentation for post-install verification
4. **Gas outlet pressure testing** is completed to NFPA 99 standards and documented for your compliance file

A non-authorized installer may complete an installation that looks correct — but lacks the factory-aligned calibration that affects performance over the 15–20 year lifecycle of the equipment.

## Preventive Maintenance: The Ongoing Relationship

The Skytron relationship doesn't end at installation. Authorized dealers provide:

- **Manufacturer-recommended PM intervals** with OEM-specified procedures
- **Genuine Skytron replacement parts** — not generic alternatives
- **Software/firmware updates** for control systems where applicable
- **Calibration documentation** that satisfies ooint Commission and DNV survey requirements

A PM program through an authorized dealer protects your warranty status. Using non-authorized service providers — even for routine maintenance — can in some cases void manufacturer warranty coverage.

## Questions to Ask Any Skytron Supplier

Before signing a purchase order or service contract, ask:

1. Are you an **authorized Skytron dealer** for Southeast Texas/our region?
2. Do your installation technicians hold **current Skytron factory certifications**?
3. Can you provide **OEM parts quickly** for emergency service situations?
4. What is your **average response time** for emergency service calls in our area?
5. Do you offer **preventive maintenance programs** with Skytron-specified intervals?

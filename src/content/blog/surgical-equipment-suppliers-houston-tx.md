Choosing the right surgical equipment supplier in Houston is more than a procurement decision — it affects OR uptime, patient safety, and the long-term performance of your facility's capital investment. Here's what hospital administrators and perioperative directors in the Houston area should look for.

## Authorized Dealer Status Matters

Not all medical equipment resellers are created equal. **Authorized dealers** receive factory training, have direct access to OEM parts, and can provide warranty-backed service. When evaluating suppliers of major surgical equipment brands like Skytron, ask directly:

- Are you an **authorized** dealer or a secondary reseller?
- Do your technicians hold **factory certifications** for the equipment brands you carry?
- Can you provide **OEM parts** on emergency orders?

Surgiquip Solutions has served as the **authorized Skytron dealer for South Texas** since the 1980s — one of a small number of dealers in the region with full factory authorization.

## Service & Repair Capability

Equipment downtime in an OR has direct clinical consequences. Houston-area facilities should evaluate suppliers on service depth:

1. **In-house biomedical technicians** — not outsourced
2. **Response time guarantees** — how fast can a tech be on-site?
3. **Preventive maintenance programs** — not just break-fix, but proactive uptime management
4. **Parts inventory** — do they stock common wear items locally?

A supplier with rapid response capability and a local parts inventory can mean the difference between a one-day and one-week OR closure.

## Capital Equipment: Buying vs. Leasing

For large-scale equipment like surgical tables, overhead lighting systems, and equipment booms, Houston hospitals have multiple acquisition paths:

- **Direct purchase** with extended warranty agreements
- **Equipment leasing** with service bundled
- **Certified pre-owned equipment** — often a cost-effective option for high-quality brands like Skytron

Ask your supplier which options they support and whether they can provide **financing assistance** or connect you with equipment financing partners.

## Installation & Integration Expertise

Modern OR equipment doesn't operate in isolation. Surgical tables, lights, ceiling-mount booms, and video systems must all coordinate. A quality supplier should offer:

- **Turnkey OR installation** — single point of accountability
- **Pre-install coordination** with your construction team
- **NFPA 99 compliance** for gas outlet and electrical commissioning
- **Staff training** at handoff — not just a manual

## Accreditation & Track Record

Look for suppliers with demonstrated accountability:

- **BBB Accredited** — indicates responsiveness to client concerns
- **43+ years in business** — longevity in a specialized market signals reliability
- **Case study portfolio** — can they show you comparable projects?
